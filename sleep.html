SLEEP

Problem

Sleep is so important for mental health. Sleep deprivation makes you tired, intolerant and exhausted. It causes your body to release stress hormones which makes you grumpy, irritable and antisocial. Research shows that people who have less than 5 hours sleep are 15% more likely to have an early death. Sleep deprivation has been proven to effect 4/10 farmers in New Zealand. This shows that sleep must have a huge impact on the farming community.

Solution
  • Get 7 to 8 hours sleep - Science has shown that your body can sustain a cycle of 16 hours awake and 8 hours asleep. Getting 8 hours sleep means that you are far less likely to feel tired and will eliminate the impact of sleep on mental health
  • Catch the “sleep wave” - Just before we go to bed our body begins to release melatonin which prepares us to sleep. If you miss the sleep wave you can release melatonin other ways such as having a hot shower. This will increase the quality of sleep.
  • Avoid stimulation - This means avoid blue light from tv screens or phones one hour before bed. This blue light triggers a switch in your brain which keeps you awake and alert. Don't drink caffeine after 3pm. Caffeine takes 7 hours to metabolise so if caffeine is taken after 3pm then it will be keeping you awake. Limit how much alcohol you drink 2 to 3 hours before bed. This will improve the quality of your sleep.
