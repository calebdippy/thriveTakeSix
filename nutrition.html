NUTRITION

Problem

Farmers always know exactly what their stock are eating and how it affects them, but not always aware of what’s going into their own bodies. Eating the right food contributes to your energy. Without energy you can feel fatigue and your mental activity becomes more negative focused. A healthy diet with good nutrition gets you performing at your best.

Solution
  • Identify good food - Eating well is all about balance. It is a good idea to learn what good, healthy food looks like. A good meal should have a balance of all the food groups. It is recommended to have a ¼ plate protein, ¼ plate carbs and ½ a plate of colourful vegetables.
  • Keep it regular - If you find yourself coming in after a hard day and feeling irritable, it may be ‘hanger’ (hungry, anger) caused by low blood sugar levels. Eat regular meals, which include some protein. If needed, snacks in between meals will keep your blood sugars constant and minimise ‘hanger’.
  • Cook once and eat twice - When you cook a meal its wise if you can make several meals at once . In no time you’ll have a freezer ‘food bank’ to use when you're under the pump. This means you can still have good meals without sacrificing extra time.
